## Overview

This project combines traditional bookbinding techniques with modern aesthetic sensibilities. The cover is crafted from vegetable-tanned leather that will develop a unique patina over time.

## Materials & Tools

- **Cover:** 3mm Vegetable-tanned leather
- **Thread:** Waxed linen, 0.8mm
- **Paper:** 100gsm acid-free cream paper
- **Tools:** Awl, needles, bone folder, edge creaser

## Process

<div class="textile-gallery">
  <img src="/assets/images/textiles/placeholder-sewing.jpg" alt="Finished leather journal with visible stitching">
</div>

1. **Pattern Cutting:** Leather pieces cut to size with precision knives
2. **Punching:** Holes marked and punched for saddle stitching
3. **Stitching:** Traditional saddle stitch using two needles
4. **Assembly:** Signatures sewn onto leather thongs
5. **Finishing:** Edge burnishing and conditioning

## Reflections

Working with textiles and leather provides a tactile counterpoint to digital engineering work. The slow, deliberate nature of hand-stitching teaches patience and attention to detail that informs all my creative practices.
